    History

    Nike's history is a tale of inspiration and perseverance. Founded in 1964 by Bill Bowerman and Phil Knight as Blue Ribbon Sports, the company rebranded as Nike, Inc. in 1971. The name "Nike" was inspired by the Greek goddess of victory, symbolizing the brand's commitment to excellence and triumph2. Nike's motivational brand strategy has been a cornerstone of its success. The company has consistently focused on inspirational storytelling, often highlighting underdog stories and overcoming adversity. One of the most notable campaigns is the "Dream Crazy" video featuring Colin Kaepernick, which resonated with many for its powerful message of standing up for one's beliefs3. The iconic "Just Do It" slogan, launched in 1988, further reinforced Nike's brand identity, emphasizing motivation, perseverance, and athletic excellence. Over the years, Nike has partnered with numerous athletes, such as Michael Jordan, Serena Williams, and Rafael Nadal, to share their stories of determination and success3. Nike's ability to connect with consumers on an emotional level through its motivational campaigns has played a significant role in its enduring popularity and global influence
